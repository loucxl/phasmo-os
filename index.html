🧠 Sanity & Hunt Chance

For most ghosts, hunt eligibility is based on average sanity of living players. Dead players are excluded. The Banshee is the big exception because it checks its current target's sanity for hunt eligibility.

🚪 Setup, Grace & Cooldown

Setup phase

  • Amateur: 5 minutes
  • Intermediate: 2 minutes
  • Professional / Nightmare / Insanity: no setup
  • Using a cursed possession immediately ends setup.

Hunt grace period

  • Amateur 5s, Intermediate 4s, Professional 3s
  • Nightmare / Insanity 2s
  • Cursed hunt 1s
  • Custom can be 0–5s

Cooldown

  • Regular hunt cooldown: 25s
  • Demon cooldown: 20s
  • Cursed hunts ignore regular sanity thresholds and cooldowns.

👻 Hunt Detection

What attracts the ghost?

  • Line of sight: the ghost can see players up to 75m if nothing blocks vision.
  • Voice: up to 9m on the same floor. Global chat within 9m can attract it even without speech.
  • Held/worn electronics: active equipment within 7.5m on the same floor.
  • Dropped electronics: do not attract the ghost, even if turned on.
  • Footsteps and door-holding noises: do not attract the ghost by themselves.
Rule of thumb: turn off or drop active electronics before hiding, stay quiet, and break line of sight before entering the hiding spot.

🧪 Newer Ghost Mechanics

Dayan

  • Within 10m, moving players make it faster and earlier-hunting: 65% threshold, 2.25 m/s.
  • Within 10m, still players make it slower and later-hunting: 45% threshold, 1.2 m/s.
  • If no player is within 10m, it behaves around standard 50% / 1.7 m/s rules.

Gallu

  • Normal: 50% threshold, 1.7 m/s, normal protection values.
  • Enraged: 60% threshold, 1.955 m/s, reduced crucifix range and shorter incense blind.
  • Weakened: 40% threshold, 1.36 m/s, better crucifix/incense protection.

Obambo

  • Starts calm, flips after 1 minute, then cycles every 2 minutes.
  • Calm: 10% threshold, 1.445 m/s.
  • Aggressive: 65% threshold, 1.955 m/s, hunts that start aggressive are 20% shorter.

Kormos

  • 50% hunt threshold.
  • 1.7 m/s normally, increasing to 2.21 m/s after detecting a player.
  • Completely blind to normal line-of-sight tracking; it detects through voice, active electronics and movement instead.
  • Movement detection depends on how you move: crouch-walking within 10m, normal walking within 15m, and sprinting within 30m.
  • If you are completely still, silent and not holding/using active electronics, it should struggle to detect you directly unless it physically paths into you.
  • Evidence overlap warning: Kormos is Orbs + Spirit Box + UV, so collecting its full evidence set does not automatically rule out The Mimic because Mimic Orbs can overlap with that combination.

🛡️ Prevention Tools

Crucifix

  • Blocks a hunt attempt if the ghost tries to start hunting inside its range.
  • Standard range varies by tier; Demon gets increased range.
  • It does not stop a hunt that has already started.

Incense

  • During a hunt, blinds/disorients the ghost long enough to break chase and hide.
  • Outside a hunt, applies a hunt-prevention timer.
  • Standard prevention is 90s; Spirit is 180s; Demon is 60s.

Firelights

  • Protect against passive sanity drain when nearby.
  • Onryo treats nearby flames like hunt blockers.
  • Every third flame blow-out can trigger an Onryo hunt if no other flame blocks it.

🔮 Cursed Possessions

Default difficulties usually spawn one cursed possession per contract, except Insanity. Activating one ends setup and can cause cursed hunts, which have a 1-second grace period and ignore normal thresholds.

Ouija BoardAnswers questions for sanity. Always say “Goodbye” before walking away.
Haunted MirrorShows the favourite room. Costs at least 20% sanity or 7.5% per second.
Music BoxReveals/summons the ghost. Being close drains sanity; careless use can start a hunt.
Summoning CircleEach candle costs 16% sanity. All five candles summon the ghost, then a cursed hunt follows.
Voodoo DollNormal pins drain 5%; heart pin drains 10% and starts a cursed hunt.
Tarot CardsHigh variance. Sun restores to 100%; Moon sets to 0%; Death starts a cursed hunt.
Monkey PawPowerful wishes with penalties. “I wish to be sane” sets sanity to 50% but increases passive drain.

📋 STRATEGY GUIDE

START HERE

New to the game?

Start by completing the in-game tutorial. It helps massively because it lets you use the main evidence tools in a safe environment before a real ghost can punish you for mistakes. Do this before your first proper contract.

Beginner goal: do not worry about perfect photos, cursed possessions, looping, or advanced ghost tests yet. Your first job is simple: find the ghost room, collect evidence, survive, and leave with the correct ghost selected.

Your first contract

  • Map: Tanglewood or Edgefield
  • Difficulty: Amateur while learning
  • Focus: evidence over objectives
  • Leave when: you know the ghost or sanity is getting low

🎓 BEGINNER RUNDOWN

01

Complete the tutorial

Use it to learn how EMF, UV, Spirit Box, Ghost Writing, D.O.T.S, Ghost Orbs and temperatures actually look when they work. This prevents a lot of false evidence calls later.

02

Choose an easy first map

Start on small houses. You want quick room checks, nearby hiding spots, and short walks back to the truck while you are learning.

03

Find the ghost room

Look for the coldest room, recent interactions, thrown objects, door touches, footsteps, EMF readings, and repeated activity in one area.

04

Set up safely

Bring evidence tools into the suspected room, keep lights on where possible, and decide where you will run before hunts become possible.

05

Confirm evidence patiently

Do not cross evidence off too early. Some tools need the right room, darkness, camera view, time, or ghost interaction before they show anything.

06

Pick the ghost and leave

Once the journal narrows to one ghost, select it and get out. Optional objectives are nice, but surviving with the correct ghost matters more.

🚪 FIRST 5 MINUTES CHECKLIST

Before opening the door

  • Check the map board and locate the breaker.
  • Look at the optional objectives, but do not obsess over them yet.
  • Take a flashlight plus room-finding tools such as EMF, thermometer, video camera, or photo camera.
  • Ask someone to remember where the nearest hiding spots are.

Once inside

  • Turn on sensible lights so sanity drains slower, but avoid turning on too many and tripping the breaker.
  • Sweep rooms quickly and listen for doors, throws, knocks, footsteps, and other interactions.
  • Mark possible ghost rooms, then return with evidence gear.
  • If you find the bone, take a photo first, then pick it up for extra reward.
On Amateur you get a setup phase where the ghost cannot start a normal hunt. Use that time to learn the map, find hiding spots, and get equipment down. Activating a cursed possession can end that safety early, so beginners should leave cursed items alone.

🔎 HOW TO FIND THE GHOST ROOM

Temperature

  • The ghost room is usually the coldest area.
  • Freezing evidence requires below 0°C / 32°F.
  • Cold breath alone is not freezing evidence.

EMF + interactions

  • Use EMF on touched doors, moved items, windows, lights and other interactions.
  • EMF 5 must be a clear level 5 reading, not just general activity.
  • Repeated EMF in one area is a strong room clue.

Sound and movement

  • Listen carefully for thrown objects and door touches.
  • Salt, motion sensors and sound sensors help on larger maps.
  • If the ghost roams, follow the pattern, not just one random event.

🧰 BASIC EVIDENCE PLAN

EMF Level 5Check real interactions. The activity board is not enough by itself.
UltravioletCheck touched doors, windows, light switches, keyboards and salt footsteps.
Freezing TemperaturesUse the thermometer. It needs to drop below freezing to count.

Beginner mistake: crossing off evidence after 30 seconds. Some evidence can take a while, especially if the room is slightly wrong, the ghost is roaming, or the tool placement is poor.

🛡️ SURVIVING YOUR FIRST HUNTS

Before a hunt

  • Know your nearest hiding spot before you need it.
  • Keep an escape path open and avoid dead-end rooms.
  • Place crucifixes around the ghost room once you know it.
  • Keep Incense and a lighter/igniter ready when sanity gets low.

During a hunt

  • Break line of sight as quickly as possible.
  • Turn off held electronics before hiding.
  • Stop talking once hidden.
  • Hold the closet/locker door closed if hiding there.
  • Use Incense if the ghost is close and you need a few seconds to escape.

After a hunt

  • Regroup outside or in the truck.
  • Check sanity and decide if another trip is worth it.
  • Restock Incense, check crucifixes, and adjust your evidence plan.
  • If you already know the ghost, leave. Greed kills more than ghosts.

👥 SIMPLE TEAM ROLES

Room Finder

  • Uses EMF, thermometer and audio clues.
  • Calls out the suspected ghost room early.

Evidence Runner

  • Brings book, D.O.T.S, cameras, UV and Spirit Box.
  • Moves gear if the ghost changes area.

Safety / Truck

  • Watches sanity, cameras and activity.
  • Brings crucifix, Incense, salt and objectives.

🏁 WHEN SHOULD YOU LEAVE?

  • Leave immediately if everyone has the ghost selected and optional objectives are risky.
  • Leave soon if sanity is low, Incense is gone, crucifixes are used, or people keep dying.
  • Stay only if you have a clear plan: one more evidence check, one safe objective, or one controlled ghost test.
  • Do not gamble for tiny rewards when the investigation is already solved.

📈 AFTER YOU KNOW THE BASICS

Learn ghost tells

  • Start noticing hunt speed, special abilities and evidence that is forced on certain ghosts.
  • Use the Nightmare / Zero Evidence tab when you are ready.

Improve your photos

  • Bone, ghost, cursed possession, interactions, salt footsteps and used crucifixes are good photo targets.
  • Take the photo before picking up the bone.

Move up difficulties

  • Once Amateur feels calm, try Intermediate, then Professional.
  • Harder modes are less about speedrunning and more about cleaner decisions.

🎲 0 Evidence Strategy

0EV is not an evidence hunt. Play it like a controlled safety puzzle: set up somewhere survivable, listen to hunts, use salt, timers and ability checks, then rule ghosts out one mechanic at a time. The only “evidence-like” exception is The Mimic's fake Ghost Orbs.

0 Evidence: no normal evidence appears, except Mimic orbs
Main tools: hunts, footsteps, salt, timers, abilities
Rule: survive first, test second
This guide if for 0 Evidence with starting Sanity. If you are playing with a starting sanity of 0, please don't follow strategies that involve sanity (Unless you have sanity pills).

🧭 The 0EV Game Plan

1. Pick a Safe Test Spot

  • Choose a hiding spot, loop, or escape route before forcing tests.
  • Drop a spare active electronic on the floor nearby so you can compare flicker range to footsteps.
  • Keep your held electronics off before and during hunts.

2. Listen to the First Hunts

  • Footstep tempo tells you base speed, speed changes, and LoS acceleration.
  • Visibility/blinking helps with Phantom, Oni, Obake, Deogen and sometimes new ghosts.
  • Do not panic-call a ghost from one hunt unless it has a hard tell.

3. Use Salt & Physical Checks

  • Salt gives fast Wraith checks.
  • Do not use UV as a test in 0EV; Obake is mainly checked by its hunt model-shift/blink tell.
  • Object throws, doors, events and candles can point toward Poltergeist, Yurei, Oni, Phantom, Mare or Onryo.

4. Confirm with Timers

  • Incense timers separate Spirit, Demon and normal ghosts.
  • Hunt thresholds separate early hunters from standard 50% ghosts.
  • Watch for behaviour changing over several minutes: Thaye, Obambo, Gallu and Mimic can shift hard.
Best habit: write down what you observed, not what you guessed. Example: “hunted above 60”, “footsteps quiet until electronic flicker”, “ignored salt”, “speed changed after smudge”.

🎒 0EV Loadout Priorities

Core Safety

  • Incense + Igniter: survival and Spirit/Demon timing.
  • Crucifix: protects test areas and reveals big Demon/Gallu/Obambo behaviour.
  • Sanity Medication: resets the pace and helps confirm Moroi curse behaviour.
  • Flashlight: drop one active on the floor for Myling range testing; do not hold it on during hunts.

Rule-Out Tools

  • Salt: Wraith and Gallu state checks. UV does not appear in 0EV.
  • Parabolic Mic: Banshee scream and Myling activity support.
  • Photo Camera: Phantom event photo test.
  • Firelight: Onryo candle test and hunt blocking.

Tracking Tools

  • Motion Sensor: tracks roams, Yurei confinement, and Goryo-like behaviour.
  • Sound Sensor / Recorder: helps compare hunt audio and Myling clues.
  • EMF Reader: catches Wraith teleports and interaction bursts.
  • Timer: essential for incense blocks and hunt cooldown patterns.

👂 Hunt Audio & Footstep Rules

How to listen safely▼
  • Normal ghost: footsteps and vocals can usually be heard from much farther away than electronic flicker range.
  • Myling: hunt sounds are only audible much closer than normal. If the dropped electronic starts flickering around the same time you first hear it, suspect Myling.
  • Revenant: slow, heavy searching steps; huge speed jump when it detects a player.
  • Deogen: very fast from far away, then crawls when close. Never hide from a confirmed or suspected Deo.
  • Hantu: speed changes with temperature. Cold areas sound dangerously fast; warmer areas sound slower.
  • Moroi: gets faster as average sanity drops. Medication can temporarily prove the difference.
  • Thaye: starts fast and dangerous, then becomes slower and less aggressive if players spend time nearby.
Do not test Myling on a different floor if you can avoid it. Floors, stairs and sound occlusion can make normal ghosts sound quieter than they really are.

🧂 Salt & Physical Tells

Wraith Check

Wraith will not step in or disturb salt. If the ghost repeatedly walks through placed salt without disturbing it, that strongly points to Wraith. If it disturbs salt, rule Wraith out.

Obake Check

In 0EV, UV fingerprints/footprints will not appear, so do not use handprints for Obake. Instead, watch hunts for the Obake model-shift/blink tell.

Gallu Salt Check

Gallu can enter an enraged state after salt, incense or crucifix use. In that enraged state, it cannot disturb salt piles. Compare whether salt works before and after provocation, but do not expect UV footprints.

Poltergeist / Yurei

Exploding item piles point to Poltergeist. A full door close with a sanity drop points to Yurei; after incense, Yurei becomes easier to contain and track.

🧪 Fast Rule-Out Flow

Hunts above 50% sanity▼
  • Very early + always dangerous: Demon, young Thaye, Raiju near electronics, Onryo after flame blow-outs, Mare in darkness.
  • Voice-based: Yokai can hunt extremely early if players talk close to it. Stay quiet and retest.
  • Movement/state-based: Dayan can become more dangerous when players move nearby. Obambo and Gallu can change thresholds depending on state.
  • Demon confirmation: 20s hunt cooldown and 60s incense prevention are much stronger than “it hunted early once”.
Speed sounds unusual▼
  • Fast with breaker on and LoS: Jinn. Cut power to confirm the speed drop.
  • Fast near active electronics: Raiju. Remove/turn off gear to confirm.
  • Slow search, huge sprint on detection: Revenant.
  • Fast cold, slow warm: Hantu.
  • Fast at distance, crawling up close: Deogen.
  • Speed changes between hunts: Twins, Moroi, Thaye, Gallu, Obambo, Mimic, or one of the newer behaviour ghosts.
Events, photos and visibility▼
  • Phantom: disappears after a successful ghost photo during an event and blinks less visibly during hunts.
  • Oni: more visible during hunts and cannot do the airball mist event.
  • Obake: can briefly shapeshift/flicker model during hunts.
  • Banshee: unique parabolic scream and target-focused behaviour in multiplayer.
  • Shade: very low activity around players and cannot start hunts with a player in the same room as it.
Incense, flame and cooldown tests▼
  • Spirit: cannot hunt for 180s after incense.
  • Demon: can hunt after 60s and has a shorter hunt cooldown.
  • Normal ghosts: use the standard incense prevention window.
  • Onryo: firelights can block hunts; repeated flame blow-outs are the tell.
  • Gallu: defensive tools can push it into a more dangerous state, then leave it weakened after the enraged hunt.

🆕 Newer Ghost 0EV Notes

These use the same behaviour data as the ghost cards, so the strategy page stays aligned with the main guide.

Dayan

Movement-sensitive. Stand still during a hunt when it is close: if it slows sharply, then speeds the moment someone moves, suspect Dayan. Also check female-only clue.

Gallu

Defensive tools and salt can change its state. Enraged Gallu is faster, has reduced incense blind time, and cannot disturb salt; after an enraged hunt it becomes weakened.

Obambo

Watch for repeating waves: calm, low-pressure periods followed by aggressive, faster, earlier-hunt periods. One weird minute is not enough; look for the cycle.

Aswang

Starts slightly slower than normal but reaches LoS speed much faster. Break sight lines quickly; long straight chases make it look scarier than its base speed suggests.

Kormos

Completely blind to normal line-of-sight, but sensitive to movement, voice and active electronics. In hunts, stay still, silent and electronics-off; then compare crouch-walking, walking and sprinting reactions at roughly 10m / 15m / 30m.

⚠️ 0EV Danger Tiers

Use this to decide how brave you should be with extra tests.

🔴 Extreme

  • Demon: early hunts, short cooldown, short incense block.
  • Deogen: hiding fails; loop only.
  • Young Thaye: fast and early before it ages.
  • Onryo: flame blow-outs can trigger hunts.

🟠 High

  • Revenant: lethal if it detects you.
  • Moroi: very fast at low sanity.
  • Raiju: electronics turn it into a sprinter.
  • Yokai: voice can cause very early hunts.
  • Obambo: safe-feeling phases can flip suddenly.

🟡 Medium

  • Jinn: power-on chase speed.
  • Mare: darker areas increase danger.
  • Hantu: cold-map speed swings.
  • Myling: quiet approach.
  • Dayan: punishes movement.

🟢 Lower

  • Spirit: predictable once timed.
  • Shade: lower pressure around players.
  • Banshee: multiplayer target can expose it.
  • Aged Thaye: much slower and less aggressive.
  • Goryo: hard to prove, but not usually mechanically lethal.
